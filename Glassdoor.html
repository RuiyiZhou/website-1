A journey I enjoyed, a job I loved, and a team I missed.

This is a brief summary of what I’ve done in this internship. The projects I’ve done are categorized into six sections: analysis, design, prototype, user research, pattern library, and mobile.

Analysis

  • I analyzed the error message of surveys using Goolge Analytics data to get an idea where and how users might be confused and make errors. Surveys are super crucial in that they influence how many reviews we get, and how good these reviews are in terms of quality.

    I also conducted a heuristic evaluation about these pages, and wrote a report about the potential usability issues these surveys have. After several rounds of refinements, it’s getting better and better, but still there is a space to improve.

Design

  • Data Visualization

    Employer Center is the B2B site to connect the employers with the protential candidates. I redesigned the data visualization pages in the EC site. One of the challenges was to design specifically for the user group of the EC site: HRs, hiring managers, etc., who didn’t necessarily have a technical background, so might be easily confused by fancy visualizations like small multiples or scatter plots.

    The activity stream

    For the smaller business especially, the data presented on the data visualizatin pages wouldn't change frequently due to the limited amount of their visitors. So users from these smaller business were less motivated to come back now and then. The challenge here was to increase the active user rate. Another more broad question was to enhumanize? the numbers, which would benefit both large and small business.

    The OpenCompany Module

    I redesigned a small but a pretty important module on the EC site homepage - the OpenCompany module - which is a certification that Glassdoor offers to encourage companies to be more responsive to their reviews and better interact with their profile viewers.

Prototype

A fully interactive prototype of the Job slots management.

User Research

Job Search Task Flow

Jobs. Jobs. Jobs. It’s all about jobs. The current user flow of job searching and application was not optimal. To start optimizing it, I worked to put together the task flows and then called out the bad experience along the task flow. And compared this experience with the ones offered by main competitors.

Day Selector

Started with putting together all the day selectors we were now using, then I started to design a day selector pattern that could be reused across the whole site. While designing this, I ran into some issues that I haven’t thought about. Trying to tackle on each of them was still a learning process. This Medium article is a brief summary of the issues I've dealt with.

Medium: https://medium.com/@ruiyizhou/how-to-build-an-ideal-day-selector-637fa3fca236

Mobile

The new Android app is trying to mirror the function we had on the iPhone app, the company tab. I started with picking up the Android design pattern and then transplant the style from iPhone to Android and twist to make it fit.
